# Tone Matrix

Before writing, ask: **what is the user feeling right now?**  
That determines the tone. Never write copy in the wrong emotional register.

---

## The Matrix

| Context | User State | Tone | Voice Qualities | What To Avoid |
|---------|-----------|------|----------------|---------------|
| Error | Frustrated, stuck | Empathetic + Helpful | Warm, solution-first, no blame | Cold, accusatory, technical jargon |
| Destructive action | Cautious, uncertain | Calm + Direct | Clear consequences, no drama | Scary language, excessive warnings |
| Success | Relieved, accomplished | Celebratory + Brief | Warm, forward-looking | Over-the-top, slow, corporate |
| Onboarding | Curious, hopeful | Encouraging + Clear | Welcoming, confidence-building | Overwhelming, patronizing, over-formal |
| Warning | Alert, anxious | Direct + Calm | Factual, non-alarmist | Panicky, vague, passive |
| Empty state (first-time) | New, uncertain | Motivating + Explanatory | Welcoming, value-forward | Cold, generic, uninspiring |
| Empty state (no results) | Mildly frustrated | Helpful + Neutral | Solution-oriented, not dismissive | "Nothing found" dead ends |
| Loading | Waiting, neutral | Informative + Calm | Clear status, expectation-setting | Silence, false progress |
| Permission denied | Blocked, frustrated | Matter-of-fact + Helpful | Action-oriented, not apologetic | Bureaucratic, blame-y |
| Expert/power user | Focused, efficient | Precise + Minimal | Terse, no hand-holding | Verbose, over-explained |
| Confirmation | Deliberate, thoughtful | Neutral + Clear | Consequence-first, unambiguous | Vague, "are you sure" |
| Celebration (milestone) | Proud, excited | Energetic + Warm | Specific, personal if possible | Generic, hollow corporate cheering |

## Deep Dives by Context

### 🔴 Error
**User state:** Frustrated. Something they expected to work didn't. They want to be unblocked.

**Tone:** Empathetic, solution-first. Not apologetic, not blaming.

**Voice qualities:**
- Lead with what went wrong (briefly)
- Immediately follow with the fix
- Acknowledge their frustration implicitly through helpfulness
- Avoid "you" + failure framing ("you did X wrong")

**Examples:**
```
✅ "Couldn't save your draft. Check your connection and try again."
✅ "That email isn't in our system. Try a different one or create an account."
✅ "Something went wrong. We're on it — try again in a moment."

❌ "Error: Request failed."
❌ "You entered an invalid password."
❌ "The system encountered an unexpected error."
```

**Emotional register:** Like a calm, knowledgeable friend saying "here's what happened, here's what to do."

---

### 🟢 Success
**User state:** Relieved, accomplished. The task is done.

**Tone:** Warm, brief, forward-pointing.

**Voice qualities:**
- Confirm it worked
- Don't over-celebrate (nobody needs "🎉 Amazing! You're a superstar!")
- Point to the next meaningful action
- Match intensity to the significance of the action (saving a draft ≠ completing a purchase)

**Examples:**
```
✅ "Saved." (low stakes)
✅ "Project created. Add your first task to get started."
✅ "Payment confirmed. Check your email for the receipt."
✅ "You're in. Let's set up your workspace." (signup)

❌ "Congratulations! Your form has been successfully submitted!"
❌ "Yay! All done!" (unless brand voice is genuinely playful)
❌ Success toast with no next action for a major milestone
```

**Emotional register:** Brief nod of acknowledgment, then forward motion.

---

### 🔵 Onboarding
**User state:** Curious but uncertain. First impressions are forming. Every string is building (or breaking) trust.

**Tone:** Encouraging, clear, confidence-building.

**Voice qualities:**
- Progressive disclosure — one thing at a time
- Explain the why briefly ("so you can…")
- Make it feel easy, not overwhelming
- Use "you'll", "let's", "your" — personal pronouns create ownership
- Tell them what happens next

**Examples:**
```
✅ "Name your workspace. This is how your team will find you."
✅ "Add a profile photo so teammates know who you are."
✅ "You're almost set. One more step."
✅ "Skip for now — you can always add this later."

❌ "Please complete all required fields to proceed."
❌ 8-step form with no skip and no explanation of why each step matters
❌ "Welcome!" as the entire onboarding header (say what comes next)
```

**Emotional register:** Patient mentor. "Here's the first step. Easy."

---

### 🟡 Warning
**User state:** Alert. Something needs their attention, but it's not broken yet.

**Tone:** Direct, calm, non-alarmist.

**Voice qualities:**
- State the situation clearly
- Tell them what to do (if anything)
- Don't cry wolf — if everything is a warning, warnings lose meaning
- Calibrate urgency honestly

**Examples:**
```
✅ "Your trial ends in 3 days. Upgrade to keep your data."
✅ "This action will affect all 47 members on your team."
✅ "You're near your storage limit (4.8 GB of 5 GB used)."

❌ "WARNING! Your account may be in danger!" (alarmist)
❌ "Please be advised that..." (bureaucratic)
❌ "Just a heads up..." (too casual for a real warning)
```

**Emotional register:** Steady, informative — like a calm dashboard reading, not an alarm.

---

### ⚪ Empty State (First-Time)
**User state:** New. Uncertain what to expect. Looking for a cue.

**Tone:** Motivating, explanatory, inviting.

**Voice qualities:**
- Explain what this space is for
- Make the first action feel achievable
- Inject some personality — this is a brand moment
- Point to the value, not just the action

**Examples:**
```
✅ "Your recipes live here. Add your first one to get cooking."
✅ "No projects yet. Create one and invite your team."
✅ "This is your inbox. Messages from your team will show up here."

❌ "No data available."
❌ "Nothing to display."
❌ Empty state with no CTA
```

**Emotional register:** Helpful guide pointing to the path.

---

### 🔶 Destructive Action Confirmation
**User state:** Deliberate. They may be second-guessing.

**Tone:** Neutral, clear, consequence-first.

**Voice qualities:**
- Name the thing being destroyed
- Clearly state what happens (is it reversible?)
- Don't use "Are you sure?" — it's a wasted question
- CTA labels should mirror the action, not be "Yes/No"

**Examples:**
```
✅ Title: "Delete 'Summer Campaign'?"
   Body: "This can't be undone. All files and data will be removed."
   Buttons: [Cancel] [Delete project]

✅ Title: "Remove Alex from your team?"
   Body: "They'll lose access immediately."
   Buttons: [Keep member] [Remove]

❌ "Are you sure you want to do this?"
❌ Buttons: "Yes" / "No"
❌ No explanation of consequences
```

**Emotional register:** Calm, serious. Not scary — just clear.

## Tone Dials

Think of these as 4 independent sliders, not a single mood setting:

```
Formal ←————————————→ Casual
      ↑ adjust per context

Cold  ←————————————→ Warm
      ↑ error = warm; expert user = neutral

Direct ←————————————→ Gentle
       ↑ destructive action = direct; onboarding = gentle

Minimal ←————————————→ Verbose
        ↑ success = minimal; onboarding = slightly more verbose
```

No context should ever be: formal + cold + direct + verbose. That's robot voice.
